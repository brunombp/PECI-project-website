Overview

Our research addresses the challenges posed by an aging population and social isolation among older people. There is a growing need for solutions that can help monitor older individuals in their own homes. Smart homes equipped with radar technology have emerged as a viable alternative, promoting active aging and independence while improving quality of life.

How It Works

System Demonstration

Our radar system continuously monitors movement patterns, recognizing five key activities: walking, sitting down, standing up, lying down, and getting up from lying position - all while maintaining complete privacy.

Methodology

Data Collection

Radar captures 3D point cloud data at a few frames per second with millimeter-wave technology.

Feature Extraction

Features extracted from a second window of data including velocity, acceleration etc...

Classification

Our algoritm classifier trained on balanced dataset achieves high accuracy in subject-independent testing using Leave-One-Subject-Out validation.

Results

Our system achieves state-of-the-art performance in activity recognition, demonstrating:

High Accuracy

Excellent performance in activity classification across different users and scenarios in subject-independent testing.

Strong F1-Score

Consistent performance in subject-dependent evaluation, showing robust recognition for individual users.

Multiple Activities

Reliable recognition of various daily movements including walking, sitting/standing transitions, and lying/getting up movements.
